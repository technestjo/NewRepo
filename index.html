?? Featured: The Aleph Journey

From a Bull's Head to the Letter A

Around 2000 BCE, Egyptian scribes drew the head of an ox to represent a sound. Canaanite workers in the Sinai mines borrowed this picture, turned it upside-down, and called it Aleph — "ox". The Phoenicians rotated it sideways. The Greeks called it Alpha. Arab scholars refined it into the elegant vertical stroke of Alef (?) — the first letter of the Arabic alphabet.
